What is New Media Art?

New media art is a genre of art that combines various art media and technology to create a unique artwork. It can range from photography to film and video to light and optics to music and much more. It can even have a combination of any or all of these. There are three main artists whose work I really enjoyed within the new media art genre. Abu Ali Mustafa, Lynn Hershman Leeson, and Nan Goldin are three artists who capture the genre of new media art very well.

The film is divided into parts. The first part shows what can be assumed to be daily life in Palestine, featuring scenes such as women cooking, doing the laundry, going grocery shopping, men sitting together and talking, or conducting business, and children playing. It shows a little girl writing a letter to her brother who I assumed was off fighting in a conflict. In the background traditional music plays throughout the video. Second part opens with a group of soldiers, with a patriotic song about Palestine accompanying the video. The letter the girl wrote is seen to be delivered to the soldier. The soldiers read their letters together while smoking cigarettes. The third part is titled “Genocide” and shows various nations and the groups those countries committed genocide against or events in which genocides happened. The video goes on to show various quotes of people who have said Palestine or Palestinians don’t exist, followed by scenes of fighter planes dropping bombs.

Abu Ali’s film has many more parts that follow, all of which show the progression of conflict and the destruction it leaves behind, and the impact it has on families and communities. The film is unforgiving in its depiction of the war in Palestine and the crimes committed against the Palestinians, who all they want is to live their lives normally like others around the world. It is a strong claiming of identity, which is what drew me to the film so much. The film can be seen here.

The next artist is Lynn Hershman Leeson. She started a work in 1973 where she began private performances as a fictional character called Roberta Breitmore. Roberta Breitmore was basically a full person with her own apartment, psychiatrist, bank account, club memberships to places like Weight Watchers, roommate ads, clothing, makeup, walk, handwriting, and much more. 144 documentations were made of this fictional person, including legal documents. At one point she multiplied into four other people appearing in her guise. In the end she held an exorcism that transformed Breitmore into the natural elements. The performance examines the work between indivuals’ “real” and “virtual” identities. The topic is more relevant than ever in today’s day and age with many people having internet identities which are different than their normal every day identities. It forces us to reevaluate between privacy, security, and convenience in a society and culture where technology plays a central role in our lives. For Leeson the project was an exploration of her own personal identity. In her own words she states that “[Roberta] was by own flipped effigy; my physical reverse, my psychological fears.” The Roberta Breitmore project explores the idea that identity is as much a socially constricted idea as a psychological one. Lastly is an artist whose work I am already familiar with, named Nan Goldin. Goldin is a photographer whose work focuses heavily on identity. She works mainly in the LGBT communities. The work I am focusing on is called Chasing a Ghost, of which the central piece is called Sisters, Saints, & Sibyls. This is a new multimedia installation that focuses on women who are trapped in psychological and mythical contexts. It explores the suicide of Goldin’s sister, Barbara, alongside the history of Saint Barbara and Goldin’s own hospitalization histories. More generally the work explores the parallel between saints and modern rebellious women. Click here to read more about the artwork.

Many of thew works that drew my attention were artists and projects which explored social turmoil or personal identity, or both. As an artist these are topics that interest me and are what in the future I would like to make and explore with my own art. Identity is an important topic for me as it is something I have struggled with my whole life, having grown up with two vastly different cultures side by side fighting for my attention. This includes cultural identity as well as national identity, as I grew up here in California but I still have strong ties to family and culture back in India. New media art is a genre that will help greatly to help me create artwork with which I can explore these themes that are important to me and help me find a way to connect with these parts of me that are conflicting.
